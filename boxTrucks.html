Direct Answer

A 26-foot box truck is a Class 5–6 straight truck with a 26-foot cargo box, capable of carrying 8,000–10,000 lbs of payload within approximately 1,700 cubic feet of enclosed cargo space. With a GVWR at or below 26,000 lbs, it requires no CDL in most states — making it the most operationally flexible heavy hauler for final-mile delivery, appliance transport, and regional freight.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

A CDL is not required for 26-foot box trucks with a GVWR at or below 26,000 lbs. If the GVWR exceeds 26,000 lbs, a minimum Class B CDL is required. Most Ryder 26-foot box trucks are spec'd at or just below the 26,000 lb threshold specifically to remain non-CDL eligible — dramatically expanding your driver pool.

A tuckunder liftgate folds and stores flush under the truck bed, allowing dock loading when not in use. It has a smaller platform (typically 48"–54" wide). A rail liftgate travels vertically along rear-mounted tracks and offers a larger, more rigid platform — ideal for very heavy pallets or bulky appliances but adds rear overhang.

Net payload on a 26-foot box truck typically ranges from 8,000 to 10,000 lbs depending on the chassis and configuration. To calculate: GVWR minus the truck's curb weight equals your net payload. Always verify the specific unit's door placard before loading — liftgate weight also counts against your payload limit.

Direct Answer

A sleeper semi truck is a Class 8 tractor with an integrated sleeping compartment (berth) designed for over-the-road (OTR) long-haul operations where drivers are away from home for multiple days. Sleeper berths range from 40 inches (day sleeper) to 82 inches (full raised-roof), and are paired with amenities including APU, refrigerator, inverter, and climate control to support federally mandated HOS rest periods.

Day Cab vs. Sleeper: Which is right for your operation?

Direct Answer

Choose a day cab for regional operations (300–500 mile radius) where drivers return home nightly. Choose a sleeper for OTR operations exceeding 500 miles or multi-day routes. Day cabs cost less and carry more payload; sleepers are mandatory for HOS-compliant long-haul where overnight stays are operationally required.

Sleeper Semi Truck FAQ

A 72-inch sleeper provides adequate rest space for OTR operations. An 82-inch raised-roof sleeper provides full standing height, more storage, room for a refrigerator, TV, and inverter — making it essentially a micro-apartment on wheels. For drivers spending 5+ nights per week on the road, the 82-inch configuration measurably improves driver retention and well-being.

Yes — for any truck used in OTR operations, an APU pays for itself within 12–18 months. Idling the main engine costs $8–$12 per hour in fuel, vs. $1.50–$3 per hour for an APU. Many states have anti-idling ordinances, and rest area parking restrictions increasingly require APU use. Ryder off-lease OTR sleepers often come APU-equipped from fleet service.

Sleeper berth drivers can split the required 10-hour off-duty period using the 7/3 split: at least 7 consecutive hours in the sleeper berth + at least 2 consecutive hours off duty/sleeper. The 7-hour period does not count against the 14-hour driving window. ELD equipment is required to log berth time accurately.
